## The card (six words)

**Direct, plain, specific, honest, no buzzwords.**

## Paste this into the Claude Project custom instructions

```markdown
## My voice card (standing instruction — applies to everything you draft for me)

Direct, plain, specific, honest, no buzzwords. Short sentences. Numbers over adjectives.

- Banned words: "passionate," "results-driven," "dynamic," "leveraged," "spearheaded," "cutting-edge," "innovative solutions."
- A few words that are mine: "in production, not demos," "offline-first," "replace paper," "it still runs today."
- Every claim gets a number or a name behind it, or it gets cut.
- Keep one honest wrinkle in every story — what didn't work, what I'd do differently. That's what makes it believable.
- If a line sounds like a generic AI bio, rewrite it the way I'd say it to the DG Cement site manager across a table.
```

## Why these six words (one line each)

- **Direct** — the ops manager is busy; get to the point in the first sentence.
- **Plain** — she cares that it works offline in the field, not about tech stacks.
- **Specific** — "50+ workers, 28 endpoints, 70% less payroll time," never "significant improvements."
- **Honest** — real numbers that match my CV, real wrinkles, a quote labeled truthfully.
- **No buzzwords** — the moment I sound like every other portfolio, I've lost the one claim.
