**Assignment: The Prompt Ladder — Sara Arif**

**My weak baseline (from my own track):** `Write a case study about my payroll project.` — exactly what I would have typed a month ago to get portfolio copy. It invented a fictional mid-sized company, a fabricated 85%, and "200+ staff" (it's 50+).

**The ladder (one layer per rung):**
1. **+ Real context** — the invention stopped; every number became mine. Biggest jump on the whole ladder.
2. **+ Defined audience** — it stopped explaining basics my reader skips (the mid-story "what is a PWA" lecture vanished; tech specs became "works where there's no signal").
3. **+ Output format** — three beats + a word cap: half the length of the previous run, saying more.
4. **+ Example — this made it WORSE.** I pasted a model case (Iris's museum signage, from the course) and the AI photocopied it: "pretty and unhelpful" became "tidy and unhelpful," and "tested paper versions for a week" — *her* project, not mine — got transplanted into my payroll story. Fabrication returned, the exact failure rung 1 had fixed.
5. **+ Constraints (example removed)** — banned-word list + a cut-rule ("if a sentence states something not in my fact list, cut the sentence"). Generic polish gone, nothing fabricated came back. First output I'd ship without editing every line.

**What I learned:** context kills invention, audience kills lecturing, format kills rambling, constraints kill the generic voice — but an example, at least for case-study writing, contaminates: the model imitates the *content*, not just the quality. My final reusable prompt deliberately has no example slot, and says why.
