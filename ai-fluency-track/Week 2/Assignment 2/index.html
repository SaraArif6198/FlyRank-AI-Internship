Rung 0 · The Baseline

No layer — a little embarrassing
The prompt
Write a case study about my payroll project.
Output — representative excerpt
"Streamlining Payroll: A Digital Transformation Success Story." In today's fast-paced business environment… this case study explores how a cutting-edge payroll management system transformed operations for a mid-sized company, reducing processing time by 85% and improving employee satisfaction across 200+ staff members. By leveraging modern web technologies, the solution delivered seamless integration…
What changed in the prompt

Nothing — this is the baseline.

What the output did

Invented everything. A fictional company, a fabricated 85%, "200+ staff" (it's 50+). No paper, no offline, no cement. Reads like every case study on the internet.

What failed

All of it: no real facts, no reader, no shape, and two words from my banned list ("cutting-edge," "leveraging").

What to try next

It can't know facts I never gave it. Biggest weakness = invention → add real context first.

Rung 1 · Layer: Real Context

+ verified fact list
The prompt — change highlighted
Write a case study about my payroll project. The facts — use only these: DG Cement payroll & attendance system, built solo by me (Sara Arif). Before: 50+ construction workers paid off handwritten paper registers at remote sites; payroll took the site manager most of a day every week; workers disputed totals. What I built: offline-first PWA (sites barely have signal), PHP/MySQL, 28 API endpoints, audit logs; I matched the 26-day payroll formula to the site manager's manual calculation by hand. Result: in production, weekly payroll time down 70%+, zero data loss during outages, I still support it today.
Output — representative excerpt
DG Cement's construction sites relied on handwritten paper registers for over 50 workers… Sara Arif architected a comprehensive offline-first Progressive Web Application leveraging PHP/MySQL with 28 API endpoints. A Progressive Web App (PWA) is a type of application that combines web and native app features… The implementation delivered a 70%+ reduction in weekly payroll processing time with zero data loss…
What changed in the prompt

One layer: real context — the verified fact list with "use only these."

What improved in the output

The invention stopped. Every number is now mine — 50+, 28, 70%, the 26-day formula. "Paper registers" and "offline" finally appear. The single biggest jump on the whole ladder.

What still failed

Written for nobody: it paused to explain what a PWA is mid-story, and the tone is a press release ("comprehensive," "leveraging" — banned again).

What to try next

Explaining basics is an audience problem → add a defined audience.

Rung 2 · Layer: Defined Audience

+ the one reader
The prompt — change highlighted
[Rung 1 prompt, plus:] The reader is one person: an operations manager at an industrial SME in Pakistan. She is drowning in paper registers and spreadsheets. She does not know or care what a PWA, an API, or PHP is — she cares whether it works in the field, what it costs her week, and whether I disappear after delivery.
Output — representative excerpt
If your sites still run attendance on paper registers, this will sound familiar. At DG Cement, attendance for 50+ workers was written by hand at remote sites, carried back, and retyped — payroll ate most of the site manager's day every week… I built them a system that works where there's no signal: attendance recorded on the spot, synced when a connection appears. Payroll that took a day now takes hours, and I still look after the system today…
What changed in the prompt

One layer: defined audience — the one ops manager, with what she does and doesn't care about.

What improved in the output

It stopped explaining basics my reader already skips. The PWA lecture vanished; "28 endpoints" became "works where there's no signal." It opens with her pain, and addressed the disappearing-freelancer fear unprompted.

What still failed

Shapeless — one river of paragraphs, ~450 words, trailed off without an ending. A busy manager won't read a wall.

What to try next

A wall of text is a structure problem → specify the output format.

Rung 3 · Layer: Output Format

+ three beats, word cap
The prompt — change highlighted
[Rung 2 prompt, plus:] Format: exactly three beats with these headings — "The problem", "What I did", "What came of it" — then one italic line starting "Next time" naming one thing I'd do differently. 150–180 words total. No introduction, no conclusion, no headline puns.
Output — representative excerpt
The problem. Attendance for 50+ construction workers lived in handwritten registers at remote sites… What I did. I built a seamless system that works with no signal… I matched the 26-day payroll formula by hand — payroll can't be off by a rupee. What came of it. A transformative outcome: payroll time down 70%+, zero records lost… Next time: measure the "before" properly so 70% isn't the only number I can defend.
What changed in the prompt

One layer: output format — three named beats + wrinkle line + hard word count.

What improved in the output

Scannable in ten seconds. The beats forced a story arc; the word cap killed the rambling; the "Next time" line produced an honest wrinkle. Half the length of Rung 2, saying more.

What still failed

The voice. "Seamless" and "transformative" crept back — generic AI polish smeared over my facts. A good copywriter, not me.

What to try next

Show it what good sounds like → paste an example of a case done right.

Rung 4 · Layer: Example — this made it WORSE

honest failure
The prompt — change highlighted
[Rung 3 prompt, plus:] Here is an example of the quality and voice I want (from my course material — Iris's museum signage case): "The problem. Museum visitors constantly stopped staff to ask where the bathrooms and exit were. The map on the wall was pretty and unhelpful. What I did. I rebuilt the map around the three questions people actually asked, cut the decoration, and tested paper versions for a week before anything was printed…" Match this quality.
Output — representative excerpt
The problem. Site managers were constantly stopped by workers asking about their pay. The paper register on the desk was tidy and unhelpful. What I did. I rebuilt payroll around the three questions workers actually asked, cut the paperwork, and tested paper versions for a week before anything went live…
What changed in the prompt

One layer: an example of a good case (Iris's museum signage, from the course).

What improved in the output

Honestly? The rhythm, slightly. That's it.

What went WRONG

It photocopied the example. "Pretty and unhelpful" → "tidy and unhelpful." Iris's "three questions" transplanted where it isn't true. "Tested paper versions for a week" — her project, not mine. Fabrication returned — the failure Rung 1 had fixed — and the case no longer could only describe MY project.

What to try next

Remove the example. Don't give it a voice to imitate — give it rules and forbid the failure modes (constraints).

Rung 5 · Layer: Constraints (example removed)

+ banned words, cut-rule
The prompt — change highlighted
[Rung 3 prompt — Rung 4's example REMOVED — plus:] Constraints: short sentences; numbers over adjectives. Banned words: seamless, transformative, passionate, results-driven, leveraged, spearheaded, cutting-edge, comprehensive. Use only the facts I gave — if a sentence states something not in my fact list, cut the sentence. Keep my phrasings where they exist: "offline-first," "replace paper," "it still runs today."
Output — representative excerpt
The problem. A cement contractor paid 50+ construction workers off paper registers… Payroll took the site manager most of a day, every week. What I did. I built an offline-first system for sites with almost no signal… I matched the 26-day payroll formula to the manager's manual calculation by hand — one rupee off starts a dispute. What came of it. Payroll time down 70%+. Zero records lost through outages. It still runs today, and I still support it. Next time: log the before-state formally, so 70% isn't the only number I can defend.
What changed in the prompt

One layer: constraints — banned-word list, numbers-over-adjectives, the cut-rule for unsupported sentences, my own phrases kept. (Rung 4's example deleted.)

What improved in the output

The generic polish is gone and nothing fabricated came back. Every banned word absent; short declaratives that sound like me. The cut-rule worked as a fabrication guard. First output I'd ship without editing every line.

What still failed

Small: it can't know facts I forgot to give (testimonial, launch date). The fact list is now the quality ceiling.

What to try next

A verification requirement — "list every fact you needed but didn't have, as questions." That's the doorway to Assignment 1's interview method; it goes in the next experiment, not smuggled in untested.

2  The Final Reusable Prompt — For a Stranger, Not Just Me

Replace the {braces}; nothing else needed. Every layer earned its rung — the example layer is deliberately absent.
Write one project as a short case study. THE FACTS — use only these; if a sentence states something not on this list, cut the sentence: {your verified facts: what existed before, what you built, the decisions you made, real numbers, current status} THE READER — one person: {who they are, what they're drowning in}. They do not know or care about {jargon they'd skip}; they care about {what they actually weigh: does it work, what does it cost them, will you disappear}. FORMAT — exactly three beats with these headings: "The problem", "What I did", "What came of it". Then one italic line starting "Next time" naming one thing you'd do differently. 150–180 words. No introduction, no conclusion. CONSTRAINTS — short sentences; numbers over adjectives. Banned words: {your banned list — start with: seamless, transformative, passionate, results-driven, leveraged, cutting-edge}. Keep these phrasings of mine where they fit: {2–3 phrases that sound like you}.

Why there is no example slot: on this ladder, pasting a model case made the output worse — the AI transplanted the example's phrases and even its facts into my project (Rung 4). Rules constrained; the example contaminated.
