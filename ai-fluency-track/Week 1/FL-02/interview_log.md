# AI Interview Log — How the Proof Statement Was Reached

> The assignment brief: *"Use AI as a thinking partner, not an author: have it interview you with sharp questions until the claim is genuinely narrow and true."*
> This log records that process. The AI (Claude) asked forced-choice questions; I answered; the AI pushed back once when my answer was still too broad.

---

## Round 1 — The three core questions

**Q1. In the next 12 months, which outcome would you genuinely be most disappointed to miss?**
Options: more freelance clients · remote junior AI/ML role · competitive internship · don't know yet
**My answer:** More freelance clients — another engagement like DG Cement: a business owner paying me to build a real system while I finish my degree.
→ *This decided the ONE person: someone who buys freelance software, not a recruiter.*

**Q2. Which single claim feels most TRUE — the one you could defend in a live interview without flinching?**
Options: A) I ship to production · B) I build AI agent systems · C) I turn data into decision tools
**My answer:** A — "I ship to production." My anchor is software real people use daily (DG Cement, 50+ workers), not notebooks or demos.

**Q3. When your ideal person finishes scrolling, what is the ONE action you want?**
Options: email a project inquiry · book a call · download CV · try a live project
**My answer:** Email me a project inquiry — a concrete scoped message. (This also matches the portfolio's Scope Builder → pre-filled contact form, which exists to produce exactly this action.)

## Round 2 — The pushback (where the claim got narrow)

The AI pushed back: **"'I ship to production' is too vague. Shipping WHAT, for WHOM?"** and offered three sharper versions:

1. **Ops systems that replace paper** — payroll/attendance/inventory systems that replace paper and spreadsheets and keep working offline in the field. DG Cement is the exact proof.
2. **Deployed AI/data tools** — broader, matches my cert stack, but my only *paying* client wasn't an AI project, so the claim would run ahead of the proof.
3. **Ops now, AI as depth** — headline the paper-replacement claim (it has the paying-client proof); show AI/ML work as supporting depth; upgrade the claim after the first paid AI project.

**My answer:** Option 3 — Ops now, AI as depth.
→ *This is the honesty move: the claim is anchored to what a client has actually paid for, not to what I'm certified in.*

**And the person, sharpened:** an **operations manager at an industrial SME** (like the DG Cement site manager) — owns a painful manual process, has budget, doesn't care about tech stacks, cares that it works offline in the field. Chosen over: early-stage founder, agency subcontractor.

## What got cut (and why)

- **Three audiences → one.** My portfolio planning doc targets recruiters + freelance clients + peers. This assignment forced a ranking: freelance client first. The others still visit; they're just not who the claim is written for.
- **Four service labels → one claim.** "AI & ML Engineer / Data Systems Architect / Full-Stack Developer / BI & Data Analyst" is four skills hiding behind "and." All still exist on the site — but only one is *the claim*.
- **"I build AI agents" as headline.** True but unproven commercially. It stays as depth until a paying client backs it.
