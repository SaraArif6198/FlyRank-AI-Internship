## The prompt (copy-paste)

Here is my portfolio sitemap — a single-page scroll site, sections in order:

1. **Hero** — states the claim ("I design and ship production business systems that replace paper processes"), availability badge, CTA button jumping to Contact
2. **Proof** — flagship DG Cement case study: paper problem → offline-first PWA → 50+ workers, 70% less payroll time, client testimonial
3. **Work** — ~6 supporting projects (including AI/data work as depth), "view all" for the rest
4. **About** — short: 2nd in CS dept, ICSC top 2%, certifications in one line
5. **Scope Builder** — interactive "what process do you want to replace?" tool that assembles a scoped inquiry
6. **Contact** — form pre-filled by the Scope Builder; the one action is: email me a scoped project inquiry

Pressure-test this map against my proof statement (in your instructions). Specifically:

- Walk through it as the ops manager at an industrial SME: where would they lose interest, get confused, or fail to reach the email action?
- Which section least earns its place, and what would you cut or merge?
- Is anything MISSING that this specific person needs before they'd email a stranger about business-critical software (e.g., trust, price fear, "is she available," "will she disappear after delivery")?
- Does the order serve the path landing → believing → acting, or should anything move?

Be blunt. End with the single highest-impact change.
