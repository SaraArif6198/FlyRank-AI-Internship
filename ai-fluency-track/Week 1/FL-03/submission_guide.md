# FL-03: Step-by-Step Submission Guide — Draw the Path: Portfolio Sitemap + Toolkit

Same workflow as FL-01: gather evidence → drop into the interactive dashboard (`index.html`) → export PDF → post.

## Step 1: Get the Sitemap Sketch Image (~10 min)

**Option A — Generate with Gemini (fits this assignment's toolkit theme):**
1. Open [gemini.google.com](https://gemini.google.com) and paste the master prompt from `gemini_sitemap_prompt.md`.
2. If text comes out garbled, reply: *"Regenerate — keep the layout but make ALL text labels large, clearly legible, and spelled exactly as written in my prompt."* (2–3 tries max.)
3. Save as `sitemap-sketch.png`.

**Option B — Draw it yourself (5 min, always safe):**
Copy the six boxes from `sitemap.md` (v2 at the bottom) onto paper. Keep the crossed-out "ABOUT" → "HOW I WORK" correction visible — it proves the pressure-test changed something. Photograph in good light.

## Step 2: Toolkit Accounts (~10 min)

Claude + ChatGPT you already have from FL-01. Add:
- **Gemini:** [gemini.google.com](https://gemini.google.com) — you'll be signed in from Step 1 anyway.
- **Perplexity:** [perplexity.ai](https://perplexity.ai) — free signup.

## Step 3: Claude Project Screenshot (~3 min)

Your project **"Portfolio Build — Sara Arif (AI Fluency, 8 weeks)"** already exists and ran the pressure-test. Just capture it:
1. Open the project in claude.ai.
2. Make sure the **project name AND the custom instructions** (with the proof statement) are visible on screen.
3. `Win + Shift + S` → save as `claude-project.png`.

## Step 4: Pressure-Test Screenshots (~5 min)

Claude's response was long — capture the **whole thread in multiple screenshots**:
1. Open the pressure-test chat in the project.
2. Screenshot top-to-bottom in overlapping sections: your prompt first, then the response in reading order.
3. **Name them so they sort correctly:** `pressure-01.png`, `pressure-02.png`, `pressure-03.png`, … (the dashboard auto-sorts by filename and numbers them "Evidence 1 of N").

## Step 5: Fill the Dashboard & Export the PDF (~10 min)

1. Double-click **`index.html`**.
2. **§4.1** — drop `sitemap-sketch.png` into the left zone.
3. **§4.2** — drop `claude-project.png` into the right zone.
4. **§4.3** — click **"Add Screenshots"** (or drag onto the wide dashed zone) and select ALL `pressure-*.png` files at once. They appear stacked and numbered; use the trash icon to remove any duplicate. Order follows filenames.
5. **§4.4** — the three changes from the pressure-test are already written in. Read them once; double-click to reword anything into your own voice.
6. Click **Print / Save as PDF** → in the print dialog:
   - Destination: **Save as PDF**
   - Paper size: **A4**, Margins: **Default**
   - More settings → ✅ check **Background graphics**
   - More settings → ❌ **uncheck "Headers and footers"** (otherwise Chrome stamps the file path/date on every page)
   - Save as: `FL-03_Portfolio_Sitemap_Toolkit_Sara_Arif.pdf`
7. **Check the PDF page plan:** p1 = title + Evaluator Summary · p2 = sitemap + cut list · p3 = toolkit + project instructions · p4+ = evidence images · last = change record + self-check. All images visible, none cut mid-image.

## Step 6: Post to Your Track Thread

Use the template in `checklist_and_thread_post.md` — attach the PDF (or the images + text, per your cohort's convention).

## What makes this version checker-friendly (nothing for you to do — just so you know)

- **Evaluator Summary on page 1:** maps each required deliverable → the exact section (§) where the evidence lives, with status pills. A grader can verify pass/revise in under a minute.
- **Revised nodes are visibly tagged** "REVISED AFTER PRESSURE-TEST" in the sitemap, so the "noted at least one thing you'll change" criterion is provable at a glance.
- **Pressure-test evidence is numbered** ("Evidence 1 of N") so a long Claude response reads in order on the PDF.
- **§4.4 lists three concrete changes**, not one — above the bar the rubric sets.
